Our Services

Individual Therapy

Personalised, one-on-one psychological support tailored to your unique needs and goals. Work with an experienced psychologist in a safe, confidential space at your own pace.

What is Individual Therapy?

Individual therapy is a collaborative process between you and your psychologist. Through regular, focused sessions you will explore the thoughts, feelings, and patterns that are keeping you stuck: and develop practical strategies to move forward. Our psychologists draw on a range of evidence-based modalities, selecting the approach most likely to work for you as an individual.

Therapy is not about being "fixed": it is about gaining insight, building skills, and reclaiming a sense of agency over your mental health. Whether you are managing a long-standing condition or navigating a difficult period of life, individual therapy provides a structured and supportive framework for real, lasting change.

Approaches We Use

Our psychologists are trained across several evidence-based therapeutic modalities and will tailor their approach to suit your presentation and preferences:

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT): identifying and challenging unhelpful thought patterns and behaviours
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT): building psychological flexibility and value-driven living
  • Schema Therapy: understanding deep-seated patterns that developed in childhood and continue to affect adult life
  • Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing (EMDR): a specialised trauma-processing therapy with strong research support
  • Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT): skills-based approach for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal effectiveness
  • Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT): integrating mindfulness practices with cognitive strategies

Who is Individual Therapy For?

Individual therapy can benefit almost anyone, at any stage of life. Our psychologists work with adults and young people (16+) presenting with a wide range of concerns, including:

  • Anxiety disorders: generalised anxiety, panic disorder, social anxiety, health anxiety
  • Depression and low mood
  • Trauma and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Grief and loss
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
  • Specific phobias
  • Stress and occupational burnout
  • Low self-esteem and perfectionism
  • Life transitions and adjustment difficulties
  • Chronic illness and pain
  • Sleep difficulties

You do not need a diagnosis or a referral to access individual therapy, though a GP referral and Mental Health Care Plan (MHCP) will allow you to access Medicare rebates (see below).

What to Expect

Your first session is an intake appointment. Your psychologist will take time to understand your history, current concerns, and what you are hoping to achieve. Together you will set goals for therapy and discuss the approach that is likely to work best for you.

Sessions run for approximately 50 minutes. Most people find that attending fortnightly or weekly sessions in the early stages yields the best outcomes. As you progress, sessions typically become less frequent. The number of sessions needed varies: some people find significant relief in as few as 6–8 sessions, while others benefit from longer-term support.

Between sessions, your psychologist may suggest worksheets, reflective exercises, or behavioural experiments to help you practise new skills in everyday life.

Funding & Fees

We believe in making quality psychological support as accessible as possible. Individual therapy at The Mood & Mind Centre can be funded through:

  • Medicare / Mental Health Care Plan (MHCP): Ask your GP for a referral and MHCP. This entitles you to a Medicare rebate of approximately $137 per session for up to 10 sessions per calendar year.
  • Private Health Insurance: Many policies include psychological services under extras cover. Check with your insurer for rebate amounts and whether a gap payment applies.
  • NDIS: We are an NDIS provider. Participants with "Improved Daily Living" funding in their plan may be able to use it for individual psychology.
  • WorkCover Queensland: Approved for WorkCover-funded psychological treatment for eligible workplace injury claims.
  • Queensland Police Service (QPS): We provide services under QPS-approved arrangements.
  • Compensation Claims: We accept referrals for motor vehicle accident and other compensation claims.
  • Private (Self-Funded): No referral required. You pay the full session fee directly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a GP referral?

No referral is required to book an appointment. However, if you would like to access Medicare rebates, you will need your GP to complete a Mental Health Care Plan (MHCP) and provide a referral to a psychologist. This is a straightforward process: simply make an appointment with your GP and ask for a Mental Health Care Plan.

How many sessions will I need?

This varies from person to person. For common presentations such as mild-to-moderate anxiety or depression, many people notice meaningful improvement within 6–10 sessions. More complex presentations, long-standing patterns, or trauma may benefit from longer-term work. Your psychologist will discuss a recommended treatment plan with you in your first session and review your progress regularly.

Is everything I say confidential?

Yes. Psychologists are bound by strict professional and legal confidentiality requirements. The only exceptions are situations involving serious risk of harm to yourself or others, or where disclosure is required by law. Your psychologist will explain these limits at your first appointment.

What is the difference between a psychologist and a counsellor?

Psychologists in Australia complete a minimum of six years of university training plus supervised practice, and are registered with the Psychology Board of Australia (AHPRA). They can diagnose psychological conditions and provide Medicare-rebatable services with a referral. Counsellors have varying levels of training and are not regulated under AHPRA. At The Mood & Mind Centre, all clinicians are registered psychologists.

Can I use Telehealth instead of attending in person?

Absolutely. All individual therapy services are available via Telehealth (secure video call). The same Medicare rebates apply. See our Telehealth page for more information.
